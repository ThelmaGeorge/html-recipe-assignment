Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

This authentic Italian pasta dish from Rome combines simple ingredients to create a creamy, luxurious sauce without using cream. The silky texture comes from perfectly tempered eggs and freshly grated cheese, making it a true testament to Italian culinary mastery.

Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 15 minutes

Number of Servings: 4 people

Difficulty Level: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti pasta
  • 200g (7 oz) guanciale or pancetta, diced
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 whole large egg
  • 100g (1 cup) freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 50g (Â½ cup) freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Salt for pasta water

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil and cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add diced guanciale and cook for 8-10 minutes until golden and crispy. Remove from heat.
  3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together egg yolks, whole egg, grated cheeses, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper until well combined.
  4. Add the hot, drained pasta to the skillet with the guanciale. Toss quickly to combine and let cool slightly for 1 minute.
  5. Remove skillet from heat completely. Slowly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta while tossing vigorously. Add pasta water gradually until you achieve a creamy, silky sauce that coats each strand.
  6. Serve immediately in warmed bowls, topped with additional grated cheese and freshly ground black pepper.

Chef's Tips

Temperature is key: Make sure to remove the pan from heat before adding the egg mixture to prevent scrambling. The residual heat from the pasta and pan will cook the eggs gently. If the sauce seems too thick, add more pasta water gradually. For the most authentic flavor, use guanciale instead of pancetta, and never add cream - the creaminess comes from the emulsified eggs and cheese!
